What I'm Building

4 goals · Sprint 1 automation plan
#1 — Top Priority
A consistent content engine publishing expert MBA admissions insight on LinkedIn and YouTube every week
~3 hrs/week freed
Content strategy LinkedIn · YouTube Claude steers
Desired outcome
Three LinkedIn posts per week and one YouTube video per month go live on a reliable schedule — built from a Claude-managed content calendar that keeps ideas aligned with audience growth and coaching positioning.
Steps to get there
  • Claude builds and maintains a rolling 4-week content calendar, proposing topics weekly based on MBA admissions cycle timing and engagement patterns

  • YouTube scripting workflow: Mike records talking points, Claude produces a structured script and optimized title/description
  • Morning news habit replaced by a 7am AI brief (5 bullets, 2 min to read) — reclaiming 2+ hrs of morning deep work for content

High leverage
mbai.app Product development Content integration

#3 — Lead Funnel
mbai.app becomes the top of a funnel that converts curious applicants into paying coaching clients
~2 hrs/week freed
Lead cultivation mbai.app funnel joinleland.com
Desired outcome
Prospective applicants discover mbai.app through content, engage with low-cost or free features, and move through a structured funnel toward intro calls and high-ticket coaching packages on joinleland.com — with Claude managing the qualification and follow-up steps automatically.
Steps to get there
  • LinkedIn InMail and inbound lead responses automated: Gmail filter → Claude agent drafts personalized reply with Calendly link → 30-second approve-and-send queue
  • Calendly booking triggers a 3-question async form (target school, timeline, budget signal); Claude scores fit and confirms or reroutes to mbai.app trial as self-service entry point
  • Intro calls batched to Tue/Thu afternoons; Claude prepares a one-paragraph brief on each prospect before the call
  • mbai.app low-cost tier (or free trial) designed as an explicit on-ramp, with in-product prompts surfacing coaching as the upgrade path
Status
Not started
#4 — Client Operations
Client messages, session scheduling, and win responses on joinleland.com run on autopilot — Mike shows up to coach, not to manage
~1.5 hrs/week freed
joinleland.com Automation Client experience
Desired outcome
The daily overhead of client communication — reading the Leland Digest, drafting responses, celebrating admitted clients, managing scheduling requests — is handled by Claude with a brief human review step, keeping every client experience warm without consuming coaching bandwidth.
Steps to get there
  • Leland Daily Digest → Claude extracts action items and drafts responses → formatted morning brief with ready-to-send text arrives by 7am; Mike reviews in 5 minutes
  • Gmail filter detects admission win patterns ("admitted," "got in," "acceptance") → Claude drafts a warm 4-sentence response referencing the specific school and client context → 10-second approve-and-send
  • Recurring session scheduling handled via Calendly automation; Claude sends prep notes to clients 24 hours before each session
  • Post-session follow-up template: Claude pulls key themes from session notes and drafts a 3-bullet recap with next steps for the client

Not started
