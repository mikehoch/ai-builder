Automation Opportunities

Ranked by impact × goal alignment  ·  DRAFT
#1 — HIGHEST IMPACT
News consumption doesn't eat your mornings anymore
3–4 hrs/week reclaimed
Quick win (under 2 hrs) Behavior change + tooling
What's happening today
drudgereport.com, mediaite, politico, foxnews, nytimes, and msn account for 634 browser visits across 15 weekdays — 30% of all work-hours browsing. This is a pull-to-refresh habit consuming ~2–3 hours daily in scattered visits.
What the automated version looks like
A daily AI brief (Claude + NewsAPI or a Substack digest) hits your inbox at 7am with 5 bullets on what matters. Site-blocking rules (1Blocker or Screen Time) restrict the news domains 9am–12pm Monday–Friday. Zero willpower required.
#2 — DIRECT FUNNEL IMPACT
Every LinkedIn InMail and inbound lead gets a qualified response within the hour — without you touching it
2–3 hrs/week reclaimed
Medium lift (half a day) Directly funds top goal
What's happening today
Nathan Shaver's InMail (MSx/EMBA, high intent) appeared in inbox Apr 16 and Apr 19 with no visible response. Ignacio Baselga required a 2-week chain before booking. Inbound interest is arriving but leaking through slow response.
What the automated version looks like
Gmail filter routes MBA-related InMails and intro requests to a Claude agent. Agent reads the message, drafts a personalized response with a Calendly link, and drops it in a "review & send" folder. You spend 30 seconds approving, not writing. High-intent leads (Wharton, Stanford, MIT signals) get priority flagging.
#3 — CONTENT ENGINE UNLOCK
Gamma content publishes to LinkedIn on a schedule without you touching it
1–2 hrs/week reclaimed
Quick win (under 2 hrs) Closes Gamma→LinkedIn gap
What's happening today
You're creating in Gamma (121 visits) but LinkedIn shows only 39 visits and no posting cadence. The creation is happening — the publishing step is the missing link. Content sits in drafts.
What the automated version looks like
Gamma export triggers a Claude reformatting step (deck → LinkedIn post in your voice). Output goes to a Buffer/Taplio queue. You review a weekly batch on Friday and hit approve. Three posts per week go live without individual effort per post.
#4 — INTRO CALL EFFICIENCY
Intro calls are pre-qualified and batched — you only talk to people worth 15 minutes
1.5 hrs/week reclaimed
Medium lift (half a day) CRM/Calendly integration
What's happening today
14 intro calls in 2 weeks at scattered times (8:15am, 5:15pm, 5:30pm, etc.) with no visible pre-qualification. Anyone on Leland can book a 15-min slot. Some are high-fit (Alessandro became a recurring client), others are unclear-intent.
What the automated version looks like
Calendly booking triggers a 3-question async form (target school, timeline, budget signal). Claude reads the form, scores fit, and either confirms the call or routes low-fit prospects to a self-service FAQ + mbai.app trial. All calls batched to Tue/Thu afternoons. You arrive to every call with a one-paragraph prep note.
#5 — DAILY OPERATIONS
Leland Digest generates your daily action list automatically — no reading required
30–45 min/week reclaimed
Quick win (under 2 hrs) Cognitive load reduction
What's happening today
The Leland Daily Digest arrives every day at 8pm. You read it and manually decide what action to take next morning. When it says "None today" you still read it. The reading + deciding step happens daily even when output is zero.
What the automated version looks like
A Gmail filter + Claude hook reads the incoming digest, extracts action items and new sessions, and pushes a formatted morning brief (with draft responses for any client messages) to your inbox by 7am. You see 1–3 specific actions with text ready to send, not a digest to process.
#6 — CLIENT EXPERIENCE
Every admitted client gets a warm, personalized response that sounds like you — without you writing it
30 min/week reclaimed
Quick win (under 2 hrs) Reputation + referral signal
What's happening today
Rishav admitted to Stanford → "Great news! Congratulations and well deserved! Mike" (5 words). Fred admitted → "That's great news! Congrats." Clients who just got into Stanford deserve more than that — and a well-crafted response is a referral trigger. You're leaving relationship capital on the table.
What the automated version looks like
Gmail filter detects subject patterns ("admitted," "got in," "acceptance," "I got the call"). Claude drafts a 4-sentence response in your voice referencing the specific school and their story context. Drops into review queue. You spend 10 seconds, hit send. Every win gets a proper celebration response.
